RBI DIGITAL BANKING GUIDELINES 2024

1. INTRODUCTION
The Reserve Bank of India issues these guidelines to regulate digital banking services and ensure consumer protection in the digital era.

2. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
Banks must maintain a minimum capital adequacy ratio of 12% to offer digital banking services.

3. DIGITAL ONBOARDING
Banks may use video-based KYC for customer onboarding subject to proper verification procedures.
Banks must implement AI-based fraud detection during digital onboarding process.

4. TRANSACTION LIMITS
Daily transaction limits for digital payments shall not exceed INR 100,000 per customer.
For first-time users, daily limits are restricted to INR 25,000 for the first 30 days.

5. SECURITY REQUIREMENTS
Banks must implement two-factor authentication for all digital transactions above INR 10,000.
Banks must implement biometric authentication for transactions above INR 50,000.

6. CUSTOMER CONSENT
Explicit customer consent is required for activating digital banking services.
Banks must obtain separate consent for data sharing with third-party service providers.

7. DATA PROTECTION
Banks must ensure encryption of all customer data in transit and at rest.
Banks must maintain data localization for all customer data within Indian jurisdiction.

8. COMPLIANCE REPORTING
Quarterly compliance reports must be submitted to RBI within 15 days of quarter end.
Monthly incident reporting for security breaches is now mandatory within 72 hours of occurrence.

9. AI GOVERNANCE
Banks using AI for customer services must ensure transparency in AI decision-making processes.
Regular audits of AI systems must be conducted quarterly.
